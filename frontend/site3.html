Mahabalipuram


Mahabalipuram, also known as Mamallapuram, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its ancient rock-cut temples and monuments. The town is renowned for its intricately carved structures, including the Pancha Rathas and Arjuna's Penance, which showcase exceptional architectural and artistic craftsmanship. It is a coastal town located along the Bay of Bengal, offering both cultural heritage and scenic beauty. Mahabalipuram was a prominent center of art, architecture, and maritime trade during the Pallava dynasty. Today, it stands as a popular tourist destination, drawing visitors from around the world to explore its historical marvels.
